Anatomy of a cherub backpack

Metamorphosis! A cherub has three identities: a student, a reporter and a weekender. Each identity requires different provisions—different bags filled with different things. A student mostly spends his or her time on campus, attending lectures and workshops. But sometimes, the student is given a task off campus in downtown Evanston, where he or she must take on the role of an on-site reporter. Afterwards, when all the hectic academic work is done, the cherub enters the last stage: a weekender. And the weekly cycle continues for five weeks.

As a student, you must be prepared with the right supplies and mindset. Know the schedule ahead of time and plan accordingly for the various lectures and workshops. Also, don’t forget to fill your backpack with little goodies that’ll help you stay awake and organized.

A backpack can help keep your things organized in one place.
A lanyard and key will come in handy when running in and out of the dorms.
A good student always has pens and pencils for taking notes.
A flash drive will make moving files from computer to computer more efficient.
Always take notes during lectures and organize your loose papers.
Having your own laptop makes note taking and completing assignments easier.
Fisk Hall and the McCormick Foundation Center can get unreasonably cold.
Chewing gum can keep you awake during long lectures.
Water can keep you hydrated between lectures.
Always keep the AP Stylebook with you to keep your writing on point.

With a multitude of assignments, a reporter must be prepared for anything, anywhere. Be observant of your surroundings and look for the best interviewees. But don’t be discouraged after a rejection—just pick yourself up and keep trying. Also, don’t forget to use your gadgets and gizmos to your aid.

A backpack can easily carry your belongings and keep them organized.
A Northwestern lanyard will help identify yourself to your interviewees.
Always take pictures of your interviewee and surroundings.
Don’t rely solely on your audio recorder—always take notes.
Pens and pencils are a necessity for note taking.
Record your interviews to get exact quotes—but don’t forget to ask for permission.
Always keep in touch with your instructor and article sources.
You never know when your phone might run out of battery.
Headphones can make transcribing easier.
You’ll be thirsty after multiple long interviews.
Eat snacks to recharge your sugar level.

Aside from a 20-minute instructor meeting in the morning, weekends are typically free for a cherub to explore the streets of Evanston. With a wide variety of restaurants and shops, the possibilities are endless. Enjoy the five weekends you have away from the stack of assignments and savor what summer is supposed to be like.

A small bag is helpful when out and about in Evanston or Chicago.
Sunscreen can protect you from getting sunburn.
Keep your money and cards tucked away in your wallet.
Use headphones to listen to music when strolling through Evanston.
Use your phone to take pictures, look at Google maps and contact your friends and family.
A portable phone charger will keep your phone up and running all day long.
You’ll need a key to re-enter your dorm at the end of a long day.
A hat protects you from the sun’s harsh rays.
An umbrella is necessary for rainy days.
Sunglasses keep you stylish and cool.
